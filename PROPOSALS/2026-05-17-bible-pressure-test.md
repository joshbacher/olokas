# Bible pressure test — May 2026

The bible was last meaningfully positioned ~12 months ago. Since then, the GEO category has had its funding moment and the surface area of "AI search" has expanded materially. Three sections of the bible read as out of date when held up against the current market.

## 1. What changed in the market

- **Profound raised $96M Series C at a $1B valuation in Feb 2026**, bringing total funding to $155M. Pricing now starts at $499/mo (Lite). Profound is no longer a peer — it is the enterprise reference. ([Fortune, Feb 24 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/02/24/exclusive-as-ai-threatens-search-profound-raises-96-million-to-help-brands-stay-visible/))
- **Peec AI raised a $21M Series A in Nov 2025**, hit $4M ARR in 10 months, 1,300+ brands, opening NYC in Q2 2026. Pricing sits at $99/mo — directly on top of Olokas's Pro tier. ([Peec AI blog](https://peec.ai/blog/we-raised-21m-series-a-to-help-brands-win-in-ai-search), [Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/peec-ai-raises-21m-series-a))
- **Google AI Overviews now appear in ~60% of U.S. queries** (Advanced Web Ranking, May 2026), up from ~31% a year earlier. Healthcare hits 88%, education 83%, B2B tech 82%. ([xponent21](https://xponent21.com/insights/google-ai-overviews-surpass-60-percent/), [ALM Corp](https://almcorp.com/blog/google-ai-overviews-surge-9-industries/))
- **The market has bifurcated by price.** Enterprise tier: Profound $499+, AthenaHQ $295–499, Scrunch $300, Semrush AI Toolkit ~$745, Ahrefs Brand Radar ~$699. Budget tier: Airefs $24, Otterly.AI $29, Olokas $39, Rankability $79, Peec $99. The middle is empty. ([Data-Mania](https://www.data-mania.com/blog/best-ai-search-tools-2026/), [TechnologyAdvice](https://technologyadvice.com/blog/sales/best-aeo-tools/))
- **Engine coverage has expanded past the four the bible names.** Competitors now routinely cover ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, Gemini, Claude, and Microsoft Copilot — six to eight engines is the new normal. AthenaHQ advertises 8+ LLMs; Ahrefs Brand Radar covers seven surfaces including AI Mode. ([AthenaHQ](https://athenahq.ai/), [Ahrefs Brand Radar](https://ahrefs.com/brand-radar))

## 2. Where the bible may need updating

### Section 1 (Identity) — add explicit budget-tier positioning

Before:

> We are not a full-stack SEO platform. We are not a content writer. We are not a website builder. We are not an agency. We do one narrow thing and we do it well: continuous AI search visibility monitoring for small business websites.

After (append a paragraph):

> We are also not a $295+/mo enterprise GEO platform like Profound, AthenaHQ, or Scrunch. The category has bifurcated into a funded enterprise tier ($295–745/mo) and a budget tier ($24–99/mo) with no real middle. Olokas owns the upper end of the budget tier — credible engine coverage and weekly cadence at small-business prices. If our entry tier ever crosses $79/mo, we have abandoned our positioning.

### Section 2 (Product) — update the engine list and add share-of-voice

Before (Starter tier):

> Weekly automated scans across all four engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude)

After:

> Weekly automated scans across six engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, Gemini, Claude). Microsoft Copilot added when its share of measured prompts justifies the engine cost.

Also under Pro tier, before:

> Slack/email alerts when ranking changes by more than X positions

After:

> Slack/email alerts when ranking changes by more than X positions. Share-of-voice tracking against named competitors (% of prompts where competitor appears vs. customer).

Rationale: every competitor reviewed reports share-of-voice as a first-class metric. Without it Olokas reads as a position tracker, which is the prior-generation framing.

### Section 5 (Acquisition) — update comparison pages and keyword cluster

Before:

> Pages every site needs: comparison pages vs Semrush, Ahrefs, WordLift, SEOptimer, Sintra. These rank well for "[competitor] alternative" queries and convert intent-laden traffic.

After:

> Pages every site needs, in priority order: (a) comparison pages vs named GEO players — Profound, Peec AI, AthenaHQ, Scrunch AI — these convert the budget-aware buyer who has rejected the $300+/mo tier; (b) comparison pages vs the SEO-suite incumbents — Semrush AI Toolkit, Ahrefs Brand Radar; (c) general SEO alternatives — Semrush, Ahrefs — for sideways category traffic. WordLift, SEOptimer, and Sintra are no longer the right comp set for AI-search-visibility intent.

Also add to the keyword cluster list: "AEO," "answer engine optimization," "AI Mode SEO," "Profound alternative," "Peec AI alternative," "cheap GEO tool," "GEO tool for small business."

## 3. Proposed action — ranked by importance

1. **Update Section 2's engine list to six engines** (highest priority). Today Olokas's product description undershoots the table-stakes feature set. A new customer comparing Olokas vs Peec at the same $99 will see "4 engines" vs "8 LLMs" and bounce. This is the single change with the most direct conversion impact, and the engineering cost is bounded (add Gemini and AI Mode adapters; Copilot can wait).
2. **Rewrite the comparison-page priority list in Section 5** (high priority). The current list is aimed at the wrong competitive set. "Profound alternative" and "Peec AI alternative" are now real, high-intent queries with budget-aware buyer intent — exactly the buyer Olokas is built for. These pages can be written this week.
3. **Add the budget-tier positioning paragraph to Section 1** (medium priority). Sharpens identity and gives every downstream piece of copy a clearer foothold. Lower urgency than the product and SEO changes because it's directional, not blocking.

Out of scope for this proposal: pricing changes (separate proposal type), adding Copilot as an engine (wait for prompt-share data), agency-tier repositioning.
