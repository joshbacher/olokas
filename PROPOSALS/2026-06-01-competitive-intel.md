## AthenaHQ

**What they shipped / changed:** AthenaHQ published their "State of AI Search 2026" report with industry-specific benchmarks and introduced the Athena Citation Engine (ACE), an ML model that predicts content citation probability. They cover 8+ LLMs including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Mode, Google AI Overviews, Claude, Copilot, and Grok. ([AthenaHQ](https://athenahq.ai/); [AthenaHQ plans](https://athenahq.ai/plans))

**Implication for Olokas:** ACE is the most substantive product move we've seen from this cohort this month — a citation-probability predictor is a genuinely useful and sticky feature. If Olokas doesn't build something like it, competitors with ML firepower will own the "what should I fix first" workflow. This is worth a future pricing-experiment proposal once we have enough scan data to train on.

## LLMrefs

**What they shipped / changed:** LLMrefs launched in January 2026 and has grown to 10,000+ marketers. It tracks 11+ AI engines including GPT-5, Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude, Grok, Copilot, Meta AI, and DeepSeek. Pricing starts at $79/mo (Pro, 50 keywords, 500 prompts/mo). ([LLMrefs](https://llmrefs.com/); [Radarkit review](https://radarkit.ai/blog/llmrefs-review/))

**Implication for Olokas:** LLMrefs covers more engines (11+) than Olokas (4), at double our Starter price. Their engine breadth is a feature Olokas has not prioritized, but it is becoming table stakes — six months ago covering 4 engines was acceptable; today competitors cite 8–11 as normal.

## Synthesis

Three trends are visible across the competitive field this month:

**1. Engine count is a hygiene metric now.** Every credible competitor covers 6–11 engines. Olokas's bible still names four (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude). The absence of Google AI Mode, Gemini, Grok, and Copilot is becoming a visible gap — not fatal today, but increasingly easy for a competitor to call out in a comparison post.

**2. The budget tier is consolidating around free tools + API.** Otterly launched a public API and 100+ workflow marketplace; Geoptie gives away six free tools; LLMrefs leads with tracking breadth. Olokas's single free scan is a minimal lead magnet by current-market standards. Adding even one additional free tool (e.g., a no-login AI citation checker for a single URL + query) could improve top-of-funnel without touching the paid product.

**3. Predictive features are coming, not arrived.** AthenaHQ's ACE citation predictor is the first genuine ML-on-scan-data feature in the market. Olokas does not have scan volume to compete here yet — but the moment we have 6–12 months of scan history per domain, this becomes worth building. Nothing to do today; flag for the product roadmap in ~Q4 2026.

**Proposed action: monitor, copy a feature, ignore, or rethink something specific.**

Recommended prioritization:
1. **Rethink engine coverage** (add Google AI Mode and Gemini to scan targets — this is a product decision, not just copy). High urgency.
2. **Copy a feature** — evaluate a second free tool alongside the single-domain scan to match the free-tier arms race. Medium urgency.
3. **Monitor** AthenaHQ's ACE predictor and Otterly's Claude Skill distribution channel. Low urgency, high signal.
4. **Ignore** Profound and Scrunch — they are enterprise products serving a different buyer.
